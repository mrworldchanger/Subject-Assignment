A Series of Apple Processor

Apple-designed processors, collectively marketed by Apple as Apple silicon, are system on a chip (SoC) and system in a package (SiP) processors designed by Apple Inc., mainly using the ARM architecture. They are the basis of Apple's iPhone, iPad, and Apple Watch platforms, and of products such as the HomePod, iPod touch, Apple TV, and AirPods. As of 2021, Apple is currently moving its Macintosh computers from Intel processors to its own processors. This switch was announced at WWDC 2020 on June 22, 2020,and the first of the ARM-based Macs, using the Apple M1 processor, were unveiled on November 10, 2020.
